DSS @ Berkeley is a group of undergraduate students at UC Berkeley focused on helping promote Data Science on campus. Our mission is to foster a data science community through educational workshops, professional events, and analytics projects.


What We Do

DSS @ Berkeley has four committees to help promote our mission:

Academic Development | Our team that hosts a wide variety of educational workshops for all skill levels open to all students on campus

Analytics | Our team that works both with corporations on consulting-type projects related to Data Science, and with professional Data Science researchers.

Career Development | Our team that organizes professional development opportunities and career events that allow UC Berkeley students to connect with industry leaders in Data Science.

Culture | Our team that mainly focuses on bringing the Data Science community at Berkeley together through social events


How You Can Get Involved

Our application season starts at the beginning of each semester. We’ll be frequently updating our website to reflect any upcoming recruiting events. There are three ways for you to be apart of the DSS @ Berkeley family:


Apply to be in a committee | Apply to be either on our Academic Development, Career Development, or Culture committees to help with the logistics of running our organization.

Apply to be on a project: | You can apply to be on one of the projects as part of the Analytics committee. This will be a semester long commitment and you’ll get to work on real-world projects.

Be a General Member | If you don’t want to be on a committee or project, you can apply to be a General Member! As a GM you’ll have priority for our workshops and discounts for some of our premium professional events.

Academic Development Committee

Directed by Heather Chen

The Academic Development Committee works on providing educational opportunities for DSS @ Berkeley members as well as anyone interested in learning about data science. Members of the Academic Development Committee teach workshops throughout the semester covering topics such as Python, SQL, R, and more. Previous teaching experience is preferred but not required.




Analytics Committee

Directed by Divyansh Agarwal

"Practice till you become perfect", a wise person once said. Truer words have never been spoken. The Analytics Committee allows UC Berkeley undergraduate students to practise data science in their quest for excellence, through the various projects and the Kaggle Team housed in the Analytics Committee. We have a wide range of projects, each using different sets of analytical approaches, including but not limited to: monte carlo simulations, statistical machine learning, deep learning, etc. We welcome students with a diversity of backgrounds and skillsets, as we believe that a diversity of perspectives is crucial to success in data science projects.


Career Development Committee

Directed by Sharon Cui & Victor Chan

The Career Development Committee aims to help students pursue a career in data science. Our two major events throughout the year are the: annual Berkeley Data Science Panel in November and Berkeley Data Science Forum in April. Both are great opportunities to get to know and talk to representatives of industry. Committee members will personally reach out to speakers, get firsthand experience in networking with them, and help DSS @ Berkeley facilitate the Career Center to host Info Sessions and potential Career Fairs. We're looking for organized, committed, and passionate people who are interested in data science to help make these events possible.


Culture Committee

Directed by Danielle Ip

The Culture Committee aims to create a fun and inclusive environment for DSS @ Berkeley members. Committee members will plan and host social events throughout the semester, like Super Smash night, Club Dinners, Ice Blocking, Study Hours, and Alumni panels. In addition, committee members will be the main drivers of the DSS @ Berkeley Family System; they will plan events for a smaller group of DSS @ Berkeley members, their "family," and facilitate their family’s bonding. The Committee will work closely with the VP of Finance and Internal Vice President to plan the two major social events of the semester: Welcome Night & New Semester Retreat and Banquet.


Decal Team

Directed by Jerry Lin

DSS @ Berkeley's DeCal team is committed to expanding the data science education for UC Berkeley students. We believe that everyone, regardless of their interests, should be given a chance to learn these skills and apply it their respective fields. The way that our DeCal team specifically strives towards this goal is through the facilitation, coordination, and execution of our semester-long DeCal. If these aspects align with yours, do not hesitate to apply.



Kaggle Team

Directed by Divyansh Agarwal

Kaggle is a data science platform that hosts a wide variety of challenging data science problems faced in industry. Data scientists from all around the world compete against each other to see who can build the better predictive model. The Kaggle team, comprised of DSS @ Berkeley, Statistics Undergraduate Student Association (SUSA), and other student organizations, will be representing our university in multiple Kaggle competitions throughout the semester and is looking for passionate, competition-driven data scientists to help us rank as high as possible.
