Berkeley Data Science Forum

Spring 2017

The Data Science Forum was an all-day, annual event hosted by the Data Science Society at Berkeley. The forum invited ten prominent speakers to share and discuss with students current revolutions in the Data Science field outside academia. In the spirit of UC Berkeley's commitment towards social justice and social responsibility, the theme for this forum is Data Science for Social Good. As the name suggests, the forum centered on exploring the ways in which available innovations in data analytics can contribute to positive social change in areas such as racial and socioeconomic equality, healthcare, and/or public safety.

Berkeley Data Science Panel 2016

Fall 2016

The Berkeley Data Science Panel is the annual data science-related career-driven event at UC Berkeley. In recent years, universities and educational institutions have been unable to update their syllabi in tune with the high speed changes taking place in the world of technology. Even entry-level jobs nowadays in industry, though not "require", still seem to prefer a PhD degree or extensive experience, leaving new undergraduates very difficult to make themselves to onsites and beyond. In Fall 2016, DSS @ Berkeley presented 5 pioneer data scienctists from the industry to share insights and give suggestions to job seekers, bridging the gap between academia and industry.
